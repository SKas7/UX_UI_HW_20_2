Feline Rescue

Providing hope to homeless cats

About the Project


Feline Rescue is a animal humane society for cats. Their goal is to shelter homeless cats and provide care for the cats in need. Our primary goal in working with them was to improve their website design and navigation to improve their online presence.

My Role


For this project, I worked in a group of three people whose names are Kina Vang and Holly Clark. My primary role in the group was to conduct UX research, ideate structure of updated website, and prototyping.

Deliverables


Our deliverable for this project was a prototype of a redesigned website for Feline Rescue.

Tools Used


Figjam, Figma, Google Drive, Miro

Timeline/Scope


This project was conducted on a 2-week sprint. After analyzing the Feline Rescue website, we focused on the adoption process of the cats.

Problem Statement


Feline Rescue was designed to achieve cat adoption/contributions. We have observed that our service is not user friendly, which is detering people’s business. How might we improve the Feline Rescue website so that our customers are more successful based on online inquiries?

Research

Competitor Analysis

There are many successful humane societies on the market at the start of this project

- We choose to analyze 3 non-profit animal societies. (Minnesota Zoo, Animal Humane Society, and the Minnesota Humane Society)

Key Insights
- Great photo galleries on home screen
- Website content was clean/organized
- Call to actions were highlighted throughout websites

Stakeholder Interviews

Interview participants were selected based on their involvment with the company (Executive Director, Board Member and a volunteer that manages their social media).


Interview Objectives

  • Understand the stakeholders vision and ambitions.
  • Learn what the stakeholders would like to portray on their website.
  • Discover what is most important to them.
  • Learn what they belive are pain points for their users.


User Interviews/Survey

We conducted 3 one-on-one interviews with our target users (21+). We also utilized a short survey to better understand our users. We then synthesized our data to create an affinity diagram.


Research Goals

From our user research, we hoped to learn the following:

  • What are people’s thoughts about donating to charitable organizations?
  • What’s important when donating?
  • What are people’s thoughts regarding adoption processes?
  • What are pain points when adopting an animal?


Research Synthesis: User Persona

Based on our affinity diagram, we defined a user persona.

User Testing

After our low-fidelity prototype was complete, we wanted to conduct user testing of our app’s design to find pain points. We conducted 4 user tests with our target demographic.

Key Findings


After organizing our user testing notes, we came together as a group to talk through many changes based on our user testing.


  • Opening page has too much white space and information hierarchy is not well executed
  • Did not like the ‘back’ button/the apply button is confusing
  • Create a description for the user when they need to input information
  • You shouldn’t need to put a down payment on the animal
  • Shipping an animal is not realistic for a humane society
  • Create a confirmation page when the user gets a receipt

Reflection

Future Opportunities

In the future, I would like to spend more time prototyping icons and aesthetics to the website to be more immersive/fun for the user. I would also like to expand the cat description page to include more visuals that include more content.

Lesson's Learned

Working with stakeholders was a very interesting aspect of this project. I learned how important it is to communicate with the stakeholders when re-designing a website. It was interesting to learn what they wanted communicated in their website so that we could help them administer them in our re-design of their website.
It was also interesting to work on this project as a group. It was challenging to keep everyone on the same page as well as come up with a decision together. I also found that with such a short time frame it can be stressful to complete all our tasks as a group. I found that communication was key to our group project as it keeps everyone on track and on the same page.
